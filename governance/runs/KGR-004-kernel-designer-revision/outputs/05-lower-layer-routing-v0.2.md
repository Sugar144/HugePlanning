# HugePlanning Lower-Layer Routing v0.2

## 1. Purpose

This register keeps operational detail below the proposed Kernel while preventing lower layers from redefining constitutional applicability. Routing describes required future work; it does not assert that a mechanism exists, is adequate, or is enforceable.

## 2. Routing principles

1. Policy defines evolving governance choices within constitutional floors.
2. Standards define shared vocabularies, schemas, records, state models, and evidence forms.
3. Procedures define concrete activity, incident, containment, recovery, and disposition steps.
4. Contracts bound a particular objective, actor, scope, data, permission, effect, budget, validation, and stop condition.
5. Configuration stores provider-, environment-, threshold-, and cadence-specific values.
6. Evaluation provides fixtures, rubrics, independent methods, semantic-fidelity assessment, and claim-specific judgments.
7. Enforcement maps clauses to prevention, detection, evidence, stopping, recovery, compensation, and escalation capabilities.
8. Research resolves empirical, legal, privacy, security, provider, cost, and usability uncertainty.
9. Repository audit and migration establish present evidence without fabricating historical compliance.
10. Every lower-layer item MUST remain traceable to its clause and MUST NOT silently narrow governed effects, human reservations, independence, evidence scope, or cumulative materiality.

## 4. Enforcement concerns preserved below the Kernel

Enforcement Engineering, only after targeted closure, must inventory and test:

- identity, delegation, expiry, revocation, and non-repudiation;
- pre-effect permission checks for state-changing and significant non-state-changing effects;
- evaluator-chain control provenance and independent evidence channels;
- claim-to-objective and claim-to-gate lineage;
- cross-actor/branch/stage/release/time aggregation;
- exception/emergency lineage and cumulative duration/effect;
- state ownership, aging, review, and terminal disposition;
- safe evidence redaction/isolation/deletion attestations;
- material-assumption dependencies and revalidation;
- usable human decision packets;
- reliable stopping and residual-effect detection;
- restoration, compensation, and irreversible-effect reconciliation; and
- scoped constitutional control coverage before any ENFORCEABLE declaration.

## 5. Boundary statement

No route authorizes implementation, declares capability, or opens Enforcement Engineering. The next function is independent targeted adversarial closure of the proposed v0.2 semantics.
