# HugePlanning Lower-Layer Routing

## 1. Purpose

This routing prevents constitutional overloading. The Kernel states stable protected properties and authority boundaries; evolving detail is assigned to the lowest layer capable of protecting those properties adequately.

Routing does not authorize implementation or imply that a listed mechanism already exists.

## 2. Routing principles

1. **Policy** defines evolving governance choices and decision rules.
2. **Standard** defines shared vocabularies, schemas, formats, criteria, and records.
3. **Procedure** defines concrete activity steps and incident or recovery sequences.
4. **Contract** bounds a particular objective, actor, scope, permission, effect, budget, validation, and stop condition.
5. **Configuration** holds provider-, tool-, environment-, threshold-, and cadence-specific values.
6. **Enforcement** maps constitutional and policy requirements to prevention, detection, evidence, stopping, and recovery mechanisms.
7. **Evaluation** supplies fixtures, rubrics, independent methods, and claim-specific judgments.
8. **Research** resolves empirical or domain-dependent uncertainty.
9. **Repository audit / S1 regularization / S2 adoption** handle transition evidence and stage readiness rather than permanent constitutional content.
10. Lower layers MUST remain traceable to the constitutional clause they refine and MUST NOT silently weaken it.
