# HugePlanning Kernel v0.1 — Constitutional Proposal

## 1. Status notice

This document is a **PROPOSED** constitutional design for HugePlanning.

It has not been ratified, does not itself grant authority, does not establish enforcement, does not declare historical compliance, and does not authorize S2 or any repository-changing work.

## 2. Preamble

HugePlanning exists to research, govern, plan, implement, validate, and evolve the system that will operate AI-assisted web-development lifecycles. Because its outputs can shape requirements, authority, evaluation, implementation, and future autonomy, HugePlanning requires a small set of stable constitutional protections.

This Kernel preserves human sovereignty, bounds delegated action, prevents self-certification, protects canonical intent and historical truth, requires evidence for state and acceptance, scales governance to real risk, and requires safe stopping and recoverable continuity. It deliberately leaves operational calibration to lower governance layers so that protection does not become permanent bureaucracy.

## 3. Scope

1. This Kernel governs **HugePlanning at system level 3**.
2. It does not automatically govern Freelancer Project at level 2, client projects at level 1, or delivered products at level 0. Application to those systems requires explicit adoption through competent authority and compatible lower-layer governance.
3. It applies to functions and effects performed within HugePlanning, regardless of whether they are carried out by a human, model, tool, script, service, session, workflow, or combination of them.
4. It governs constitutional properties. Policies, standards, procedures, contracts, configurations, evaluations, and enforcement mechanisms MAY refine these properties but MUST NOT contradict or silently weaken them.

## 4. Interpretation rules

### 4.1 Authority and precedence

Upon ratification, authority MUST be interpreted in this order:

1. Ratified Kernel.
2. Explicit current human decisions made within constitutional authority.
3. Current policies.
4. Stage and task contracts.
5. Canonical repository documentation.
6. Agent and skill instructions.
7. Local execution interpretation.

A lower layer MAY specialize a higher rule but MUST NOT contradict it. An ordinary instruction MUST NOT amend the Kernel. A same-level source supersedes another only when applicability, version, and replacement are explicit or otherwise demonstrable. An unresolved same-authority conflict MUST remain `BLOCKED` and be escalated.

### 4.2 Normative language

- `MUST` and `MUST NOT` express constitutional requirements or prohibitions.
- `SHOULD` and `SHOULD NOT` express strong expectations that require recorded justification when departed from.
- `MAY` expresses constitutionally permitted discretion; it does not itself provide operational authorization.

### 4.3 Functional interpretation

Proposal, decision, authorization, execution, validation, acceptance, and ratification are distinct functions. One person or technical component MAY perform more than one function when permitted by criticality and conflict-of-interest controls, but labels, separate chats, or repeated executions alone do not establish meaningful independence.

### 4.4 Technology independence

Constitutional meaning MUST remain independent of any named provider, model, tool, command, repository layout, prompt, or implementation technique. Technology-specific behavior belongs below the Kernel. Substituting a tool or provider SHOULD NOT require constitutional redesign unless the substitution changes authority, protected effects, or constitutional guarantees.

### 4.5 Honest state and uncertainty

`BLOCKED`, `PAUSED`, `INCONCLUSIVE`, `READY_FOR_EVALUATION`, `ACCEPTED_WITH_LIMITATIONS`, and `ABRUPT_TERMINATION` are legitimate states when their conditions are honestly met.

`READY_FOR_EVALUATION` is not `ACCEPTED`. `INCONCLUSIVE` is not `PASS`. The absence of a detected problem is not proof of compliance, authorization, or acceptance.

### 4.6 Materiality and cumulative effect

A matter is material when it can change committed intent, authority, mandatory criteria, criticality, affected assets, external impact, acceptance, propagation, or recoverability. Materiality MUST be assessed over the cumulative related effect, not only over individual files, tasks, commits, or executions.

### 4.7 Constitutional violation handling

A suspected constitutional violation MUST block any dependent state-changing transition that would rely on the disputed authority, evidence, or result. Relevant state and evidence MUST be preserved, the issue MUST be escalated to competent authority, and acceptance or release MUST NOT be inferred. Containment MAY proceed under K-007. A violation does not automatically invalidate unrelated work that can be shown to remain independent and conformant.

## 5. Constitutional clauses

## K-001 — Constitutional Sovereignty and Governed Amendment

### Normative statement

Only competent human constitutional authority MAY ratify or amend this Kernel, transfer ratification authority, change the authority hierarchy, redefine HugePlanning's fundamental purpose or scope, increase maximum autonomy or permissions, reduce constitutional validation or security protections, or accept serious, critical, or constitutional residual risk where human judgment is required.

The system MAY analyze and propose such changes, but it MUST NOT authorize, ratify, conceal, or enact them through ordinary policy, contracts, implementation, emergency action, or its own outputs. Every amendment MUST be explicit, versioned, impact-assessed, independently challenged, considered for enforceability and migration, and humanly ratified before it acquires constitutional authority.

### Rationale

Constitutional legitimacy fails if the governed system can enlarge its own authority, weaken the standards applied to it, or convert an emergency or ordinary instruction into an amendment.

### Scope

- Kernel ratification, amendment, and supersession.
- Authority hierarchy and transfer of constitutional authority.
- Fundamental purpose, scope, architecture, maximum autonomy, and permissions.
- Constitutional protections and serious, critical, or constitutional residual-risk acceptance.
- Emergency actions that could otherwise be mistaken for permanent governance change.

### Protected properties

- Human sovereignty.
- Constitutional legitimacy.
- Controlled evolution.
- Non-self-amendment.
- Preservation of meaningful human control.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-002 — Self-certification or criterion weakening.
- HZ-008 — Autonomy outruns control.
- HZ-012 — Sensitive or external effect without adequate controls.
- HZ-018 — False retrospective compliance.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-002 bounds delegated execution. K-003 prevents interested self-approval. K-004 and K-005 preserve the basis and evidence for amendment. K-006 determines proportional review without reducing the constitutional floor. K-007 permits containment but not amendment by emergency.

### Constitutional violation effect

An unratified constitutional change has no constitutional authority. Dependent execution, acceptance, delegation, or release MUST be `BLOCKED`; the prior valid constitutional state remains controlling unless and until competent human ratification occurs.

### Exception posture

No ordinary exception is allowed. Temporary containment MAY suspend automation, revoke permissions, isolate components, or restore a safer state under K-007, but it MUST NOT amend the Kernel, permanently weaken protections, delete evidence, or create indefinite risk acceptance.

### Review triggers

- Any proposed Kernel amendment or supersession.
- Transfer of ratification authority.
- Fundamental purpose, scope, or architecture change.
- Increase in maximum autonomy, permissions, or external-effect capacity.
- Proposed reduction of constitutional validation or security protections.
- Acceptance of serious, critical, or constitutional residual risk.

## K-002 — Bounded Authority and Effects

### Normative statement

Every state-changing action MUST be covered by explicit, current, and competent authorization that bounds its objective, target, scope, permissions, permitted effects, conditions, criticality, validation obligations, and stop or escalation boundary.

An actor MUST NOT expand, reinterpret, transfer, or reuse authorization after a material basis has changed, and MUST NOT increase its own permissions or lower its own criticality. Silence, missing controls, technical capability, prior success, or possession of credentials MUST NOT be treated as authorization. An unknown, unclassified, or unauthorized state change MUST remain `BLOCKED` while safe read-only investigation MAY continue when permitted.

### Rationale

Capability is not authority. Without constitutional boundaries, local instructions, standing access, or technical affordances can silently expand into systemic or external effects.

### Scope

- All state-changing work and all authorization-bearing functions.
- Canonical artifacts, code, configuration, schemas, evaluators, workflows, permissions, releases, external systems, sensitive data, and resource commitments.
- Standing and delegated authorization.
- Material scope changes and unknown action classes.

### Protected properties

- Bounded authority.
- Least necessary privilege and effect.
- Explicit delegation.
- Controlled externality.
- Safe escalation.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-008 — Autonomy outruns control.
- HZ-011 — Unsafe automerge.
- HZ-012 — Sensitive or external effect without adequate controls.
- HZ-016 — Critical change fragmented into low-risk pieces.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-001 defines non-delegable constitutional powers. K-003 constrains who may validate or accept authorized work. K-006 determines proportional control and cumulative classification. K-007 defines the duty to stop when authority becomes insufficient.

### Constitutional violation effect

The affected action and every dependent state transition MUST be treated as unauthorized and `BLOCKED`. Any effects already produced MUST be contained, preserved for review, and recovered or explicitly dispositioned by competent authority before acceptance.

### Exception posture

A temporary deviation MAY be authorized only by the competent owner of the governing rule, with explicit scope, rationale, risk, compensating controls, expiry or review condition, and a record of actual effects. It MUST NOT waive K-001, permit interested self-approval under K-003, or convert silence into consent.

### Review triggers

- New actor, capability, permission, autonomy level, or external-effect class.
- Material scope or authorization change.
- Reuse of standing authorization under changed conditions.
- Unclassified action or disputed criticality.
- Unauthorized effect, credential exposure, or repeated exception request.

## K-003 — Functional Independence and Non-Self-Certification

### Normative statement

The functions of proposal, decision, authorization, execution, validation, exception approval, acceptance, and ratification MUST remain distinguishable and MUST be separated to the degree required by criticality, conflict of interest, and consequence.

A benefiting or materially interested executor MUST NOT unilaterally define or weaken the blocking criteria, lower the criticality, approve an exception, provide the decisive independent evaluation, accept its own critical work, or ratify its own constitutional proposal. Claims of independence MUST consider shared context, criteria, evidence, methods, tools, incentives, and authority; nominal separation alone is insufficient.

### Rationale

A system can appear reviewed while reusing the same assumptions, oracle, incentives, or authority. Meaningful functional independence prevents circular approval without requiring a permanent organization or a different person for every low-risk task.

### Scope

- Criterion ownership and modification.
- Criticality classification and reduction.
- Validation, evaluation, exception approval, acceptance, release, and ratification.
- Critical implementation and constitutional design.
- Independence claims across humans, models, tools, sessions, and methods.

### Protected properties

- Evaluation legitimacy.
- Conflict-of-interest control.
- Non-self-certification.
- Independent risk acceptance.
- Separation of constitutional functions.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-002 — Self-certification or criterion weakening.
- HZ-003 — Premature completion.
- HZ-007 — Correlated evaluation.
- HZ-008 — Autonomy outruns control.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-001 reserves ratification and high-risk authority. K-005 defines evidence-grounded results and acceptance. K-006 scales the degree of separation proportionally. K-004 preserves criteria and provenance so that changes cannot be hidden.

### Constitutional violation effect

A validation, exception, acceptance, release, or ratification outcome produced through prohibited self-certification MUST NOT be relied upon. The result MUST return to an honest non-accepted state and be reviewed under criteria and authority not controlled by the benefiting executor.

### Exception posture

Functions MAY be consolidated for low-criticality work when policy demonstrates that no material conflict exists and required objective gates remain outside the benefiting executor's unilateral control. No exception permits self-ratification, self-approved critical exceptions, or unilateral acceptance of serious, critical, or constitutional risk.

### Review triggers

- Criterion or rubric change following validation failure.
- Same function fixes, evaluates, and accepts a critical result.
- Evaluators share a material oracle, context, source, or incentive.
- Criticality reduction proposed by the executor.
- Exception requested by its beneficiary.
- New delegation of acceptance or release authority.

## K-004 — Canonical Intent, Provenance, and Epistemic Integrity

### Normative statement

Governed intent, decisions, requirements, criteria, evidence, assumptions, and state MUST have identifiable canonical authority and traceable provenance. Facts, human decisions, proposals, assumptions, inferences, open questions, evidence gaps, and validation results MUST remain distinguishable and MUST NOT be silently promoted, merged, rewritten, or represented as one another.

Competing authoritative sources MUST be reconciled through applicability, version, and explicit supersession; unresolved equal-authority conflict MUST remain visible and `BLOCKED`. Historical evidence and compliance MUST NOT be fabricated retroactively, and source evidence MUST be preserved when summaries, migrations, or learning artifacts are created.

### Rationale

HugePlanning can build the wrong system convincingly if inferred intent, stale summaries, or reconstructed history acquire silent authority. Canonical and epistemic integrity protects every downstream decision.

### Scope

- Requirements, decisions, assumptions, evidence, criteria, states, hazards, defects, and learning.
- Canonical artifacts and derived summaries or views.
- Versioning, supersession, migration, and historical regularization.
- Conversations and temporary working material used as supporting evidence.

### Protected properties

- Intent preservation.
- Canonical truth.
- Provenance and traceability.
- Assumption integrity.
- Historical honesty.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-001 — Intent or requirement corruption.
- HZ-006 — Loss of provenance or canonical authority.
- HZ-015 — Assumptions silently become permanent facts.
- HZ-018 — False retrospective compliance.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-005 relies on preserved evidence and criteria. K-002 relies on canonical authorization. K-001 relies on an authentic amendment record. K-007 preserves state and evidence across interruption and recovery.

### Constitutional violation effect

A dependent decision, implementation, evaluation, or acceptance MUST NOT proceed while a material canonical conflict, provenance failure, or epistemic misclassification remains unresolved. The disputed material MUST be marked, preserved, and escalated without inventing a reconciliation.

### Exception posture

Temporary copies, summaries, caches, or working views MAY exist only when their noncanonical status and canonical reference are clear. No exception permits fabricated evidence, silent supersession, deletion of material source evidence, or promotion of an assumption or inference into a human decision.

### Review triggers

- Conflicting or duplicated authoritative sources.
- Missing provenance for a material decision, criterion, or result.
- Assumption expiry, refutation, or stage transition.
- Migration or retrospective regularization.
- Summary or generated artifact used as canonical authority.
- Evidence deletion, replacement, or unexplained history change.

## K-005 — Evidence-Grounded State and Acceptance

### Normative statement

Every material claim, validation outcome, completion state, acceptance decision, release decision, and declaration of compliance MUST be supported by evidence sufficient for the specific claim, criticality, uncertainty, and decision authority involved.

Artifact existence, creator assertion, structural validity alone, or absence of detected failure MUST NOT establish purpose satisfaction. `READY_FOR_EVALUATION` MUST precede acceptance where evaluation is required; `INCONCLUSIVE` MUST NOT be treated as `PASS`; missing or contradictory essential evidence MUST remain explicit and MUST block the affected gate. Acceptance MUST be made only by the competent authority and MUST preserve known limitations and residual risk.

### Rationale

Without claim-specific evidence and honest states, generated artifacts and superficial checks become false proof, causing invalid work to propagate through the roadmap.

### Scope

- Validation, evaluation, acceptance, release, compliance, and operational-status claims.
- Completion and state transitions.
- Retrospective evidence and migration status.
- Qualified acceptance and residual-risk records.

### Protected properties

- Evidence integrity.
- Honest completion state.
- Claim-specific validation.
- Acceptance legitimacy.
- Non-fabricated compliance.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-002 — Self-certification or criterion weakening.
- HZ-003 — Premature completion.
- HZ-007 — Correlated evaluation.
- HZ-014 — Rollback is assumed but ineffective.
- HZ-018 — False retrospective compliance.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-003 supplies functional independence. K-004 protects evidence provenance and criteria. K-006 determines proportional evidence depth. K-007 ensures interrupted or recovered work cannot acquire acceptance accidentally.

### Constitutional violation effect

The affected result MUST NOT cross its gate or retain an unsupported accepted state. It MUST be returned to `READY_FOR_EVALUATION`, `INCONCLUSIVE`, `BLOCKED`, `FAILED`, or another honest non-accepted state until competent review establishes a justified outcome.

### Exception posture

`ACCEPTED_WITH_LIMITATIONS` MAY be used only by competent authority when limitations, scope, residual risk, owner, and review condition are explicit and no mandatory constitutional control or core purpose is violated. Missing essential evidence, unacceptable serious risk, core-purpose failure, invalid downstream dependence, or fatigue MUST NOT be converted into qualified acceptance.

### Review triggers

- Gate crossing based on file existence or creator assertion.
- Contradictory or insufficient evidence.
- `INCONCLUSIVE` result or qualified acceptance proposal.
- Change to mandatory criteria, test, rubric, or evidence source.
- Retrospective compliance or reversibility claim.
- Declaration of `RATIFIED`, `ENFORCEABLE`, `OPERATIONAL`, or `MATURE`.

## K-006 — Proportional Governance and Justified Complexity

### Normative statement

Governance, authorization, validation, independence, observability, stopping, recovery, and human review MUST be proportional to the actual, cumulative, uncertain, and potentially external effects of the governed work.

Related actions MUST be classified and controlled by their combined intent, capability, release, affected system, and propagation, and MUST NOT be fragmented to evade stronger controls. Material uncertainty MUST use the higher reasonable provisional level until evidence supports reduction. Every added governance component or burden MUST demonstrate protective value over a simpler adequate alternative and SHOULD be reduced, lowered, or removed when it adds cost without preserving necessary protection.

### Rationale

Uniform maximum control makes a solo system unusable, while local classification enables critical effects to hide inside small tasks. Proportionality protects both safety and delivery.

### Scope

- Criticality, aggregation, uncertainty, and control selection.
- Validation depth, independence, human gates, and recovery requirements.
- Governance artifacts, agents, skills, workflows, reviews, and permanent components.
- Calibration and simplification of lower-layer controls.

### Protected properties

- Proportional control.
- Anti-fragmentation.
- Honest uncertainty.
- Solo maintainability.
- Justified complexity and anti-overplanning.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-004 — Overplanning and governance inflation.
- HZ-005 — Context, usage, or budget exhaustion.
- HZ-008 — Autonomy outruns control.
- HZ-010 — Unjustified agent and workflow proliferation.
- HZ-016 — Critical change fragmented into low-risk pieces.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-001 fixes non-delegable constitutional floors. K-002 applies proportional authorization to effects. K-003 scales independence. K-005 scales evidence. K-007 scales observability and recovery. K-006 MUST NOT be used to waive another clause's essential protection.

### Constitutional violation effect

Work suspected of under-classification, fragmentation, or materially uncertain effect MUST be provisionally governed at the higher reasonable level and MUST NOT cross the affected gate until reviewed. Governance found to be unjustifiably burdensome SHOULD be simplified at the appropriate lower layer without weakening required protection.

### Exception posture

Lower layers MAY calibrate levels, thresholds, examples, and mechanisms. No exception permits hiding cumulative effect, treating material uncertainty as certainty, or using cost alone to waive K-001 through K-005 or K-007. Emergency response MAY temporarily apply stronger controls without amending the Kernel.

### Review triggers

- New effect class, actor, capability, autonomy level, or external dependency.
- Related low-risk changes with a larger cumulative outcome.
- Criticality dispute or repeated reclassification.
- Controls repeatedly bypassed, ineffective, or more costly than their protective value.
- Proposal for a permanent agent, workflow, document class, or governance platform.
- Serious incident, repeated defect, or material `INCONCLUSIVE` result.

## K-007 — Safe Stopping, Observability, Continuity, and Recovery

### Normative statement

State-changing work MUST stop, pause, or remain `BLOCKED` before further effect when required authority, canonical input, classification, context, essential evidence, control capacity, or recovery basis is absent, conflicting, exhausted, corrupted, or materially uncertain, or when continuation would require violating a higher rule. Safe read-only investigation MAY continue when authorized and when it does not increase the disputed effect.

Significant work MUST externalize sufficient state and evidence to reconstruct its objective, authority, inputs, changes, validations, current status, limitations, and exact continuation or recovery boundary. Recovery and reversibility claims MUST be evidence-based and proportional to risk. Abrupt termination, ambiguous residual state, or failed recovery MUST NOT imply acceptance and MUST trigger preservation, classification, containment, and authorized resumption, compensation, or reversion.

### Rationale

A system that cannot stop safely, reconstruct what happened, or recover from interruption cannot maintain bounded authority or trustworthy evidence, especially under finite context, time, cost, and human attention.

### Scope

- Stop, pause, block, interruption, checkpoint, resumption, containment, and recovery.
- Significant state-changing work, external effects, long executions, and resource-bounded work.
- Observability and reconstruction of authorization, changes, validation, and final state.
- Abrupt termination and failed rollback or compensation.

### Protected properties

- Safe stopping.
- Continuity and resumability.
- Observability and reconstructability.
- Evidence-preserving containment.
- Proportional recovery.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-001 — Intent or requirement corruption.
- HZ-005 — Context, usage, or budget exhaustion.
- HZ-008 — Autonomy outruns control.
- HZ-012 — Sensitive or external effect without adequate controls.
- HZ-013 — Abrupt termination leaves ambiguous state.
- HZ-014 — Rollback is assumed but ineffective.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-002 supplies the authorization boundary that triggers stopping. K-004 and K-005 define the canonical and evidential state to preserve. K-006 scales observability and recovery. K-001 limits emergency containment so that it cannot become amendment.

### Constitutional violation effect

Further dependent state change MUST cease. Written or external effects MUST be treated as unvalidated until reconstructed and reviewed; relevant evidence MUST be preserved; containment and recovery MUST be initiated under competent authority. A failed recovery claim MUST be treated as an incident rather than hidden or relabeled as success.

### Exception posture

Immediate containment MAY precede normal workflow authorization when necessary to stop active harm, but it MUST use the least effect reasonably available, preserve evidence where possible, be recorded and reviewed, and MUST NOT authorize constitutional amendment, permanent control weakening, evidence deletion, or indefinite risk acceptance.

### Review triggers

- Abrupt termination or missing terminal state.
- Exhausted or unreliable context, time, cost, compute, or execution budget.
- Missing checkpoint or inability to reconstruct significant work.
- Failed rollback, recovery, compensation, or restoration claim.
- New sensitive or external action class.
- Incident, unexplained state, or repeated pause/block without progress.

## 6. Amendment relationship

1. This proposal MAY be challenged, revised, rejected, or ratified only through the governance sequence appropriate to constitutional work.
2. Before ratification, it MUST receive independent adversarial review and enforcement analysis sufficient to expose ambiguity, bypasses, costs, and migration consequences.
3. A future amendment MUST identify the affected clauses, alternatives, hazards, compatibility effects, lower-layer consequences, migration needs, and evidence used.
4. Ratification creates constitutional authority only for the explicitly approved version. Later policies, contracts, tools, or emergencies MUST NOT be treated as implicit amendments.
5. A superseded Kernel version and its amendment record MUST remain traceable under K-004.

## 7. Adoption boundary

Ratification, enforceability, operation, and maturity are separate states:

- `RATIFIED` means competent human constitutional approval.
- `ENFORCEABLE` means minimum prevention, detection, evidence, stopping, or recovery mechanisms exist.
- `OPERATIONAL` means the Kernel has been used successfully in a real governed flow and declared operational by competent human authority using independent evidence.
- `MATURE` means accumulated evidence supports stability, effectiveness, and reasonable cost.

This document currently has none of those states. Its sole constitutional status is:

```text
PROPOSED — NOT RATIFIED
```
