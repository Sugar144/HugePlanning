## 4. Constitutional scope

The proposal governs **HugePlanning at level 3**: the system that researches, governs, plans, implements, validates, and evolves Freelancer Project.

It does not automatically govern Freelancer Project at level 2, client projects at level 1, or delivered products at level 0. Those systems require explicit adoption and compatible lower-layer governance.

## 5. Binding design constraints preserved

The proposal preserves these owner decisions:

- Human constitutional authority is final.
- Ordinary instructions cannot override a ratified Kernel.
- Authority, permissions, scope, and effects are bounded.
- Proposal, decision, authorization, execution, validation, acceptance, and ratification are distinct.
- Interested critical work cannot certify itself or control the criteria that block its own acceptance.
- Canonical intent, provenance, evidence, assumptions, and uncertainty remain distinguishable.
- Artifact creation is not evidence of purpose satisfaction.
- `READY_FOR_EVALUATION` is not `ACCEPTED`; `INCONCLUSIVE` is not `PASS`.
- `BLOCKED`, `PAUSED`, qualified acceptance, and abrupt termination remain honest states.
- Control is proportional to actual and cumulative effect.
- Material uncertainty uses the higher reasonable provisional criticality.
- C3/C4 acceptance remains human as established by the intake model.
- Safe stopping, resumability, observability, and recovery are legitimate requirements.
- Complexity must justify itself against a simpler adequate alternative.
- Constitutional meaning remains technology-independent.
- Historical evidence and compliance must not be fabricated.
- S0a–S1 regularization remains retrospective and proportional.
- S2 remains a future governed pilot after ratification and minimum executable governance.
- Governance must remain understandable and maintainable by a solo owner.

## 7. Constitutional design principles

1. **Protect properties, not implementations.** Constitutional text states stable outcomes and authority boundaries rather than tools, commands, schemas, thresholds, or layouts.
2. **Separate distinct authority semantics.** Protections are combined only when they share compatible authority, violation effects, and enforcement direction.
3. **Prefer lower layers for calibration.** Criticality examples, evaluator maps, checkpoint fields, scenario fixtures, and control checklists remain below the Kernel.
4. **Preserve honest uncertainty.** Missing evidence produces explicit uncertainty, `BLOCKED`, `PAUSED`, or `INCONCLUSIVE`, not invented completion.
5. **Control cumulative effects.** Constitutional classification cannot be bypassed by fragmenting one material change.
6. **Prevent self-weakening.** The system may propose evolution but cannot increase its authority, amend its constitution, or weaken acceptance protections through ordinary work.
7. **Minimize governance burden.** Every constitutional obligation must protect a systemic asset and justify its cost for a solo-maintained system.
8. **Interpret by function and effect.** Protections do not depend on permanent agent names or organizational form.
