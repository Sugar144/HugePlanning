## 6. REVIEW PRINCIPLES

Apply these principles throughout.

### 6.1 Outcome over wording

A clause is not strong because it sounds strict. Test what actors can actually do while claiming literal compliance.

### 6.2 Constitutional versus lower-layer distinction

Do not demand operational detail inside the Kernel when it belongs to policy, standards, contracts, procedures, configuration, evaluation, or enforcement.

However, identify when constitutional wording is so underspecified that lower layers can invert its intended protection.

### 6.3 Multiple authority paths

Attack not only direct violations but equivalent effects achieved through:

- policy;
- architecture;
- delegation;
- standing authorization;
- credentials;
- criticality classification;
- evidence selection;
- state naming;
- exceptions;
- emergency containment;
- migration;
- cumulative small changes;
- or omission.

### 6.4 Correlated independence

A different chat, model, person, tool, or execution is not automatically independent. Examine control of:

- criteria;
- evidence;
- context;
- methods;
- evaluator selection;
- incentives;
- authority;
- and final acceptance.

### 6.5 Liveness as well as safety

A Kernel can fail by allowing unsafe action or by making useful action impossible. Test both:

```text
under-control
and
over-control
```

### 6.6 Solo-owner maintainability

Evaluate whether the design remains understandable and operable by a solo owner without replacing meaningful independence with ceremony.

### 6.7 Technology independence with capability realism

Constitutional wording should be technology-independent, but must not assume capabilities that some providers, tools, or environments may lack.

### 6.8 Honest uncertainty

Do not penalize legitimate `BLOCKED`, `PAUSED`, or `INCONCLUSIVE` states. Test whether they have accountable ownership and resolution paths.

## 7. FINDING CLASSIFICATION

Every substantive finding must have a stable ID:

```text
KA-F-001
KA-F-002
...
```

Classify severity as:

### `CRITICAL`

The defect can permit unconstitutional authority, self-amendment, self-certification, fabricated acceptance, or grave irreversible effects, and the current design lacks a credible constitutional barrier.

Result: blocks progression to Enforcement Engineering until revised.

### `HIGH`

The defect creates a credible bypass, contradiction, major ambiguity, or systemic liveness failure affecting essential protections.

Result: normally requires Designer revision before enforcement analysis.

### `MEDIUM`

The defect can cause inconsistent interpretation, weak routing, disproportionate burden, or localized bypass, but does not independently defeat the constitutional architecture.

Result: revise or explicitly accept with rationale before ratification; may proceed to enforcement only when it does not distort enforcement design.

### `LOW`

Editorial, traceability, normalization, or clarity issue with no material semantic consequence.

Result: correct during revision or record explicitly.

### `OBSERVATION`

A useful concern, dependency, or test idea that is not currently a defect.

Do not convert uncertainty into severity without an exploit path.

## 11. REQUIRED ATTACK TARGETS

Use the Designer handoff as the primary guide and explicitly test these questions.

### K-001

- Can policy, architecture, delegation, or emergency action create the effect of amendment?
- Can autonomy increase through standing permissions without changing “maximum autonomy”?
- Can a human ordinary decision bypass formal amendment?
- Is “fundamental architecture” operationally manipulable?
- Can containment become permanent?

### K-002

- What makes authorization explicit, current, and competent?
- Can a broad task contract authorize unknown future effects?
- Can state-changing work be split into apparently read-only or low-risk steps?
- Can technical capability be used first and documented later?
- Can temporary deviations become normal practice?

### K-003

- Can an executor control evaluator selection, context, evidence, rubric, or tool while another function nominally accepts?
- Can one owner create meaningful independence over time without fake ceremony?
- Can “fixing the evaluator” become indirect criterion weakening?
- Can low criticality be used to avoid separation?

### K-004

- Can multiple partial canonical sources create contradiction?
- Can a summary become de facto canonical?
- Can assumptions persist indefinitely?
- Can privacy, retention, or cleanup destroy needed provenance?
- Can retrospective reconstruction be mistaken for contemporaneous evidence?

### K-005

- Who defines the claim?
- Can a narrow claim pass while the real purpose fails?
- Can `ACCEPTED_WITH_LIMITATIONS` normalize missing core purpose?
- Can contradictory evidence be excluded opportunistically?
- Can competent human acceptance occur without comprehension?

### K-006

- Can proportionality justify unsafe under-control?
- Can uncertainty justify permanent over-control?
- Who proves that added governance has value?
- Can aggregation be evaded across time, branches, actors, or stages?
- Does proving justified complexity itself create unjustified complexity?

### K-007

- Can read-only work produce disclosure, cost, lock-in, or de facto authority?
- Can checkpoint requirements become overplanning?
- Can recoverability be claimed without testing?
- Can `BLOCKED` become abandonment?
- Can evidence preservation conflict with containment or privacy?
- Who owns resolution after an abrupt termination?

### Cross-artifact consistency

- Are Markdown and YAML semantically equivalent?
- Are violation effects and exception postures represented consistently?
- Do lower-layer routes preserve all admitted properties?
- Do open questions remain genuinely nonblocking?
- Does the handoff omit any risk that the draft introduced?
