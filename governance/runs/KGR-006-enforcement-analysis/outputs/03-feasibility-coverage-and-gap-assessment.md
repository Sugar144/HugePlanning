# Feasibility, Coverage, and Gap Assessment

## Coverage summary

All seven clauses are analyzed once at clause level. LLR-001 through LLR-019 are applicable to the analysis; LLR-020 is accounted for as `NOT_APPLICABLE_TO_GOV_5_EXECUTION`, with GOV-8 as its later destination. The matrix derives 20 requirements and supplies prevention, detection, evidence, stopping, recovery/compensation, feasibility, burden, residual-risk, and reserved-human-decision implications for every clause effect family.

Coverage here means analytical accounting, not demonstrated control capability. Zero of the 20 requirements has complete bound capability evidence; seven are partial, nine are gaps, three depend on specialists, and one depends on provider testing.

## Feasibility limits

- Human competence, comprehension, freedom from pressure, and substantive judgment cannot be established by record presence alone.
- Fundamental architecture classification, materiality, relatedness, evidence sufficiency, and semantic fidelity retain contextual human or independent judgment.
- Complete discovery of hidden effects, assumptions, conflicts, correlated reviewers, or strategically fragmented work is not feasible from repository records alone.
- Provider stop, audit, export, retention, deletion, isolation, permission, context, and recovery behavior needs empirical scope-specific evidence.
- Jurisdictional legal/privacy duties and secure least-loss evidence handling cannot be inferred without the actual data, purpose, location, provider, and threat context.
- Repository restoration cannot reverse disclosure, communication, spent cost, external reliance, or decisions; recovery must distinguish restoration from reconciliation or compensation.
- Operational effectiveness, false positives/negatives, solo-owner burden, and monetary value cannot be measured before a bounded implementation and pilot.

## Cost and burden

The minimum path limits future evidence and controls to one explicit governed transition and its actual effect classes. Expensive work—independent evaluation, provider tests, recovery rehearsal, specialist analysis, and usability review—is trigger-gated. Deterministic identity, schema, hash, inventory, and parity work should reuse existing scripts. Sampling and operational burden assessment belong after use; GOV-5 supplies no invented hours, prices, effectiveness scores, or return-on-investment claims.

The main burden risk is turning 20 routed concerns into a platform or document suite. The recommendation instead groups them into a small transition contract, evidence/authority basis, independent gate, managed state/stop path, recovery boundary, parity/coverage evidence, and human decision packet.

## Gaps and unsupported scope

The nine direct gaps are ER-002, ER-004, ER-005, ER-006, ER-011, ER-014, ER-017, ER-018, and ER-020. Until later satisfied, the affected claims remain blocked. The package does not support live external/sensitive effects, provider equivalence, critical self-independent evaluation, real-data lifecycle compliance, general recovery effectiveness, or any scoped capability declaration.

Historical S0a-S1 audit and regularization remain excluded under LLR-020 and routed to GOV-8. Multi-tenant isolation, billing, enterprise identity, commercial SLA, complete provider coverage, a full policy suite, and product/runtime implementation are outside the current analysis and recommendation.

## Scalability retrofit risks

- Provider-specific assumptions embedded without explicit profiles would make later substitution unsafe; retain provider-neutral requirements and provider-specific evidence.
- Non-exportable evidence or canonical records would create lock-in; retain portable file formats, hashes, and explicit provenance.
- Mixed project data or authority scope would obstruct future separation; bind project, transition, data, and effect scope explicitly even in single-user operation.
- Identity records that equate one user with all governance functions would make later role separation costly; record functions and authority independently of current staffing.

These are analysis constraints only. They do not authorize commercial, multi-project, or multi-user implementation.
