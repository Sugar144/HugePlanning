# KGR-006 Independent-Evaluation Handoff

## Trigger and input boundary

This handoff becomes usable only after KGR-006 has been separately authorized, executed, deterministically validated, and imported into review custody with exactly seven outputs. Preparation does not trigger evaluation. The evaluator receives the immutable KGR-006 prompt, envelope, input package, output contract, exact seven outputs, validator evidence, and this handoff.

## Independence boundary

The evaluator must be appointed by the Project Owner or another competent human outside the Enforcement Engineer's unilateral control. The evaluator must not be the author of the decisive KGR-006 analysis and must disclose material conflicts. The Engineer may answer factual questions but may not select the evaluator, determine the evaluation criteria after seeing results, suppress dissent, accept findings, accept risk, or ratify.

No second large formal process is prepared here. The later evaluator may use a bounded review contract proportionate to the seven-output package. Any material correction returns to a versioned KGR-006 correction or separately allocated run; evaluation does not silently rewrite the Engineer's output.

## Required evaluation dimensions

The evaluator must assess:

- exact coverage of K-001 through K-007 and LLR-001 through LLR-019;
- fidelity to the closed Kernel without expansion, weakening, or reinterpretation;
- evidence-to-claim scope and honest capability classifications;
- visibility of gaps, infeasibility, cost, burden, residual risks, and blocked claims;
- correct trigger-gated specialist treatment and safe interim boundaries;
- separation of requirements from candidate mechanisms;
- minimum-scope discipline and analysis-only scalability treatment;
- material parity between the YAML matrix and six Markdown outputs;
- whether the ratification handoff is comprehensible without implying acceptance or enforceability.

## Evaluator authority limits

The evaluator may report findings and a bounded recommendation. The evaluator may not modify Kernel meaning, accept risk, ratify, declare enforceability, implement GOV-7, activate a later phase, or allocate authority to itself.

## Decision handoff

The evaluated package goes to the competent human ratification owner. The human may return, reject, or separately ratify the exact proposed Kernel under GOV-6. No evaluation outcome performs that decision.
