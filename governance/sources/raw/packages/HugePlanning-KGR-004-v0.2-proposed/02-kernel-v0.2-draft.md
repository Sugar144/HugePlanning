# HugePlanning Kernel v0.2 — Constitutional Proposal

## 1. Status notice

This document is a **PROPOSED** constitutional revision produced by KGR-004 from the preserved KGR-002 baseline and the independent KGR-003 adversarial findings.

It has not been ratified, independently closed, made enforceable, adopted, or declared operational. It grants no execution authority, does not open Enforcement Engineering, and does not establish historical compliance.

## 2. Preamble

HugePlanning exists to research, govern, plan, implement, validate, and evolve the system that will operate AI-assisted web-development lifecycles. Its outputs can shape requirements, authority, evaluation, implementation, external effects, and future autonomy. The Kernel therefore preserves human constitutional sovereignty, bounds all constitutionally significant effects, prevents direct and indirect self-certification, protects canonical and epistemic integrity, binds evidence to real authorized purpose, scales governance without weakening fixed floors, and requires safe stopping with accountable continuity and recovery.

Operational calibration remains below the Kernel. Lower layers may make these protections executable but may not redefine when they apply.

## 3. Scope

1. This Kernel governs HugePlanning at system level 3.
2. It does not automatically govern Freelancer Project at level 2, client projects at level 1, or delivered products at level 0; those systems require explicit adoption by competent authority and compatible lower-layer governance.
3. It applies by function and governed effect, regardless of whether a human, model, tool, script, service, session, workflow, or combination performs the work.
4. Policies, standards, procedures, contracts, configurations, evaluations, and enforcement mechanisms may refine constitutional properties but must not contradict, silently weaken, or redefine their applicability.

## 4. Interpretation rules

### 4.1 Authority and precedence

Upon ratification, authority MUST be interpreted in this order:

1. Ratified Kernel.
2. Explicit current human decisions made within constitutional authority.
3. Current policies.
4. Stage and task contracts.
5. Canonical repository documentation.
6. Agent and skill instructions.
7. Local execution interpretation.

A lower layer MAY specialize a higher rule but MUST NOT contradict it, silently weaken it, or decide that a constitutional reservation is unnecessary. An ordinary instruction, including an ordinary human instruction, MUST NOT amend the Kernel. A same-level source supersedes another only when applicability, version, and replacement are explicit or otherwise demonstrable. An unresolved same-authority conflict MUST remain BLOCKED and receive accountable disposition under K-007.

### 4.2 Normative language

MUST and MUST NOT express constitutional requirements or prohibitions. SHOULD and SHOULD NOT express strong expectations that require recorded justification when departed from. MAY expresses constitutionally permitted discretion and does not itself grant operational authorization.

### 4.3 Functional interpretation

Proposal, decision, authorization, execution, validation, evaluation-chain control, exception approval, acceptance, release, and ratification are distinct functions. One person or component MAY perform more than one function when criticality and conflict controls permit, but labels, separate chats, repeated runs, or different tools alone do not establish meaningful independence.

### 4.4 Technology independence

Constitutional meaning MUST remain independent of named providers, models, tools, commands, prompts, repository layouts, and implementation techniques. Substitution does not require constitutional redesign unless it changes authority, governed effects, or constitutional guarantees; no substitution may be treated as control-equivalent without capability evidence for the applicable effect classes.

### 4.5 Honest state and uncertainty

BLOCKED, PAUSED, INCONCLUSIVE, READY_FOR_EVALUATION, ACCEPTED_WITH_LIMITATIONS, FAILED, and ABRUPT_TERMINATION are legitimate non-success or qualified states when their conditions are honestly met. READY_FOR_EVALUATION is not ACCEPTED; INCONCLUSIVE is not PASS; FAILED is non-accepted. Absence of a detected problem is not proof of compliance, authorization, acceptance, or recoverability.

### 4.6 Governed effects

A governed effect includes a state change and any constitutionally significant non-state-changing effect, including sensitive access, collection, disclosure, or external transmission; material resource or cost commitment; external communication or exposure; and an output that acquires canonical, authorization, evaluation, acceptance, or release authority. Genuinely harmless local reading or analysis may receive lighter controls when it creates none of those effects.

### 4.7 Materiality and cumulative effect

A matter is material when it can change committed intent, authority, mandatory criteria, criticality, protected assets, external impact, acceptance, propagation, control capability, or recoverability. Materiality MUST be assessed over related effects across actors, branches, stages, releases, systems, and time; subdivision or a new record does not reset cumulative effect.

### 4.8 Managed nonprogress states

Every material BLOCKED, PAUSED, INCONCLUSIVE, or higher-provisional state MUST identify an accountable owner, reason, affected dependency, and a next review condition or explicit terminal disposition. Safety may require continued blocking without a forced deadline to proceed, but the state MUST NOT become unowned abandonment.

### 4.9 Constitutional violation handling

A suspected constitutional violation MUST block every dependent governed transition or effect that relies on the disputed authority, evidence, result, or control. Relevant state and evidentiary integrity MUST be preserved under K-004, the issue MUST be escalated and owned, and acceptance or release MUST NOT be inferred. Containment MAY proceed under K-007. Unrelated work that is demonstrably independent and conformant is not automatically invalidated.

## 5. Constitutional clauses

## K-001 — Constitutional Sovereignty and Governed Amendment

### Normative statement

Only competent human constitutional authority MAY ratify or amend this Kernel; transfer ratification authority; change the authority hierarchy; redefine HugePlanning’s fundamental purpose or scope; approve a fundamental architecture change when it materially changes authority concentration, maximum autonomy or permissions, governed-effect pathways, canonical-control topology, evaluation or acceptance topology, or constitutional guarantees; increase maximum autonomy or permissions; reduce constitutional validation or security protections; make final acceptance decisions for C3 or C4 work; or accept serious, critical, or constitutional residual risk. A lower layer MUST NOT redefine, relabel, automate, or narrow any reserved category so as to remove this human authority. Changing a reserved boundary requires constitutional amendment. The system MAY analyze and propose such changes, but MUST NOT authorize, ratify, conceal, or enact them through ordinary policy, architecture, contracts, implementation, emergency action, classification, or its own outputs. Every amendment MUST be explicit, versioned, impact-assessed, independently challenged, considered for enforceability and migration, and humanly ratified before acquiring constitutional authority.

### Rationale

Constitutional legitimacy fails if the governed system can enlarge its authority, decide that a human-only gate is unnecessary, or create equivalent constitutional change through architecture, classification, emergency, or ordinary work.

### Scope

- Kernel ratification, amendment, supersession, and transfer of constitutional authority.
- Fundamental purpose and scope.
- Architecture changes only when they materially alter authority, autonomy, governed-effect pathways, canonical or evaluation topology, or constitutional guarantees.
- Maximum autonomy, permissions, constitutional protections, C3/C4 final acceptance, and serious/critical/constitutional residual-risk acceptance.
- Temporary or emergency arrangements whose cumulative effect could become constitutional.

### Protected properties

- Human sovereignty.
- Constitutional legitimacy.
- Controlled evolution.
- Non-self-amendment.
- Meaningful human control.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-002.
- HZ-008.
- HZ-012.
- HZ-018.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-002 bounds delegated effects. K-003 prevents indirect or interested self-approval. K-004 and K-005 preserve the authentic basis and informed evidence for amendment and acceptance. K-006 cannot lower this clause’s floors. K-007 permits containment but not de facto amendment.

### Constitutional violation effect

An unratified constitutional change or non-human exercise of reserved authority has no constitutional force. Dependent execution, acceptance, delegation, release, or continuing governance MUST remain BLOCKED; the prior valid constitutional state controls until competent human ratification or disposition.

### Exception posture

No ordinary exception is allowed. Immediate containment MAY reduce effects under K-007, but it MUST NOT amend the Kernel, exercise reserved acceptance authority, permanently weaken protections, or become normal governance through serial renewal.

### Review triggers

- Proposed Kernel amendment, supersession, or transfer of ratification authority.
- Change to fundamental purpose or scope.
- Architecture change with a material effect on authority, autonomy, governed-effect pathways, canonical or evaluation topology, or guarantees.
- Increase in maximum autonomy, permissions, or external-effect capacity.
- Reduction of constitutional validation or security protections.
- C3/C4 final acceptance or acceptance of serious, critical, or constitutional residual risk.
- Continuing exception or emergency state with cumulative constitutional effect.

## K-002 — Bounded Authority and Effects

### Normative statement

Every action capable of a governed effect MUST be covered by explicit, current, and competent authorization proportional to that effect and bounding its objective, target, scope, permissions, data and resources, permitted effects, conditions, criticality, validation obligations, and stop or escalation boundary. This requirement applies to state changes and to constitutionally significant non-state-changing effects. An actor MUST NOT expand, reinterpret, transfer, or reuse authorization after a material basis or effect class changes, and MUST NOT increase its own permissions or lower its own criticality. Broad standing language, silence, missing controls, technical capability, prior success, or possession of credentials MUST NOT absorb an unknown or materially changed effect. Unknown, unclassified, or unauthorized governed effects MUST remain BLOCKED. Genuinely harmless local investigation MAY continue only when permitted, bounded, and not increasing the disputed or protected effect.

### Rationale

Capability and syntactic read-only status are not authority. Sensitive access, transmission, cost, external exposure, or authoritative outputs can be consequential without mutating the target system.

### Scope

- All state-changing work and all constitutionally significant non-state-changing effects.
- Canonical artifacts, code, configuration, schemas, evaluators, workflows, permissions, releases, external systems, sensitive data, external communications, and resource commitments.
- Outputs that acquire governed authority or become a basis for decision, acceptance, or release.
- Standing and delegated authorization, material scope changes, and unknown action or effect classes.

### Protected properties

- Bounded authority.
- Least necessary privilege and effect.
- Explicit delegation.
- Controlled externality.
- Safe escalation.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-008.
- HZ-011.
- HZ-012.
- HZ-016.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-001 defines non-delegable powers. K-003 constrains decisive evaluation and acceptance control. K-006 aggregates related effects. K-007 stops work when authorization or control is insufficient.

### Constitutional violation effect

The affected action or effect and every dependent transition MUST be treated as unauthorized and BLOCKED. Effects already produced MUST be contained, recorded, and recovered, compensated, or explicitly dispositioned by competent authority before any acceptance.

### Exception posture

A temporary deviation MAY be authorized only by the competent owner of the governing rule, with explicit scope, rationale, risk, compensating controls, an objectively bounded termination condition, review conditions, cumulative lineage, and actual-effect record. Renewal MUST be assessed cumulatively and MUST NOT reset duration or effect. A deviation cannot waive K-001, permit prohibited self-approval under K-003, or convert silence into consent.

### Review triggers

- New actor, capability, permission, autonomy level, governed-effect class, data class, or external destination.
- Material scope, authorization, data, cost, or effect change.
- Standing authorization reused under changed conditions.
- Unclassified action/effect or disputed criticality.
- Unauthorized effect, credential exposure, broad read-only claim, or repeated/renewed deviation.

## K-003 — Functional Independence and Non-Self-Certification

### Normative statement

Proposal, decision, authorization, execution, validation, evaluator appointment, claim and rubric ownership, evidence selection, context construction, method selection, decisive interpretation, exception approval, acceptance, release, and ratification MUST remain distinguishable and MUST be separated to the degree required by criticality, conflict of interest, and consequence. For critical or materially conflicted work, a benefiting or materially interested executor MUST NOT unilaterally control the decisive evaluation chain, including the evaluator, gate claim, blocking criteria, fixtures or evidence set, context, method, interpretation, exception, and acceptance. The executor MUST NOT lower criticality, weaken criteria directly or indirectly, approve its own exception, accept its own critical work, or ratify its own constitutional proposal. Independence claims MUST consider shared context, criteria, evidence, methods, tools, incentives, selection authority, and final decision authority; nominal separation alone is insufficient.

### Rationale

A separate evaluator identity does not prevent self-certification when the beneficiary controls what is evaluated, by whom, with which evidence and method, and how the result is interpreted.

### Scope

- Criterion, fixture, evidence-source, claim, and rubric ownership or modification.
- Evaluator appointment, context, methods, evidence inclusion, and decisive interpretation.
- Criticality classification and reduction.
- Validation, exception approval, acceptance, release, ratification, and critical implementation.
- Independence claims across humans, models, tools, sessions, and methods.

### Protected properties

- Evaluation legitimacy.
- Conflict-of-interest control.
- Non-self-certification.
- Independent risk acceptance.
- Separation of constitutional functions.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-002.
- HZ-003.
- HZ-007.
- HZ-008.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-001 reserves human constitutional and C3/C4 authority. K-004 preserves criteria and evaluation-control provenance. K-005 binds evidence to the real gate and requires informed reserved acceptance. K-006 scales separation without waiving the critical floor.

### Constitutional violation effect

A validation, exception, acceptance, release, or ratification outcome produced through prohibited beneficiary control or correlated self-certification MUST NOT be relied upon. It MUST return to an honest non-accepted state and be reviewed through a decisive chain outside the beneficiary’s unilateral control.

### Exception posture

Functions MAY be consolidated for low-criticality work when policy demonstrates no material conflict and objective gates remain outside the beneficiary’s unilateral control. No exception permits self-ratification, self-approved critical exceptions, beneficiary-controlled decisive evaluation of critical work, or non-human exercise of K-001 reserved authority.

### Review triggers

- Criterion, fixture, evidence source, claim, rubric, context, or method change after failure or execution begins.
- Executor selects or materially constrains the decisive evaluator.
- Same function fixes, evaluates, and accepts critical work.
- Evaluators share a material oracle, context, source, method, incentive, or authority.
- Criticality reduction or exception proposed by its beneficiary.
- New delegation of acceptance, release, or evaluator-selection authority.

## K-004 — Canonical Intent, Provenance, and Epistemic Integrity

### Normative statement

Governed intent, decisions, requirements, criteria, claims, evidence, assumptions, evaluation-control records, and state MUST have identifiable canonical authority and traceable provenance. Canonical authority MAY be composite or distributed only when authoritative boundaries, composition, and conflict resolution are explicit. Facts, human decisions, proposals, assumptions, inferences, open questions, evidence gaps, retrospective reconstructions, and validation results MUST remain distinguishable and MUST NOT be silently promoted, merged, rewritten, or represented as one another. Competing authoritative sources MUST be reconciled through applicability, version, material-fidelity evidence, and explicit supersession; unresolved equal-authority conflict MUST remain visible and BLOCKED. A summary or migration MUST NOT replace a primary source as canonical authority unless material intent and provenance are demonstrably preserved. Material assumptions supporting governed effects or acceptance MUST have an accountable owner and a validity or revalidation condition; materially stale, contradicted, or expired assumptions MUST block dependent gates. Historical evidence and compliance MUST NOT be fabricated retroactively. Evidentiary integrity and the maximum safe provenance MUST be preserved. Competent legal, privacy, security, or active-harm authority MAY require documented least-loss redaction, isolation, minimization, cryptographic or equivalent attestation, or deletion of raw evidence, but the reason, scope, authority, timing, confidence impact, and safest available decision record MUST remain traceable.

### Rationale

Canonical truth fails both when evidence is silently rewritten and when absolute raw retention creates unlawful or dangerous persistence. Integrity requires provenance, semantic fidelity, current assumptions, and accountable least-loss handling.

### Scope

- Requirements, decisions, assumptions, claims, evidence, criteria, states, hazards, defects, evaluation-control records, and learning.
- Canonical artifacts, composite authorities, primary sources, derived summaries, views, and migrations.
- Versioning, supersession, semantic-fidelity evidence, retrospective reconstruction, retention, redaction, isolation, minimization, and deletion.
- Conversations and temporary working material used as supporting evidence.

### Protected properties

- Intent preservation.
- Canonical truth.
- Provenance and traceability.
- Assumption currency and integrity.
- Historical honesty.
- Safe evidence lifecycle.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-001.
- HZ-006.
- HZ-012.
- HZ-015.
- HZ-018.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-005 relies on canonical objectives, criteria, claims, and evidence. K-002 relies on canonical authorization. K-001 relies on authentic amendment and human-decision records. K-007 preserves or safely handles state and evidence during interruption and containment.

### Constitutional violation effect

A dependent decision, implementation, evaluation, acceptance, release, or governed effect MUST NOT proceed while a material canonical conflict, provenance failure, stale material assumption, unsafe evidence condition, or epistemic misclassification remains unresolved. Disputed material MUST be marked and handled without invented reconciliation; confidence loss from redaction or deletion MUST remain explicit.

### Exception posture

Temporary copies, summaries, caches, and working views MAY exist when their status and canonical reference are clear. Least-loss evidence handling MAY occur only under competent authority and documented necessity. No exception permits fabricated evidence, silent supersession, unrecorded cleanup, concealment of evidence loss, or promotion of an assumption, inference, or retrospective reconstruction into a contemporaneous human decision or fact.

### Review triggers

- Conflicting or duplicated authoritative sources or composite-authority ambiguity.
- Summary, generated artifact, or migration proposed as canonical authority.
- Missing provenance or semantic-fidelity evidence for a material decision, criterion, claim, or result.
- Material assumption approaching or exceeding its validity/revalidation condition, or becoming contradicted.
- Retrospective reconstruction or historical regularization.
- Evidence redaction, isolation, deletion, replacement, unexplained loss, or privacy/security conflict.

## K-005 — Evidence-Grounded State and Acceptance

### Normative statement

Every material claim, validation outcome, completion state, acceptance decision, release decision, compliance declaration, reversibility claim, and adoption-state declaration MUST be supported by evidence sufficient for the canonical authorized objective, the exact gate claim, accepted scope, mandatory criteria, material dependencies, criticality, uncertainty, and decision authority involved. A narrow claim MAY receive a narrow PASS, but MUST NOT imply broader completion, compliance, release, or acceptance. Artifact existence, creator assertion, structural validity alone, or absence of detected failure MUST NOT establish purpose satisfaction. READY_FOR_EVALUATION MUST precede acceptance where evaluation is required; INCONCLUSIVE MUST NOT be treated as PASS; missing or materially contradictory essential evidence MUST remain explicit and block the affected gate. Acceptance MUST be made only by competent authority. Where K-001 reserves human acceptance, the decision MUST be informed and attributable: the accepted scope, material evidence, limitations, contradictory evidence or dissent, and residual risk MUST be presented in a comprehensible decision basis. Known limitations and residual risk MUST remain attached to the accepted scope.

### Rationale

Strong evidence for an underscoped claim or a ceremonial human click can still produce false completion. Gate legitimacy requires traceability from purpose to claim, evidence, authority, and accepted scope.

### Scope

- Validation, evaluation, acceptance, release, compliance, reversibility, recovery, and adoption-state claims.
- Completion and state transitions, local versus gate-level PASS, and material dependencies.
- Retrospective evidence and migration status.
- Qualified acceptance, recurring limitations, human decision basis, and residual-risk records.

### Protected properties

- Evidence integrity.
- Honest and scoped completion state.
- Purpose-to-claim traceability.
- Informed acceptance legitimacy.
- Non-fabricated compliance.

### Hazards addressed

- HZ-002.
- HZ-003.
- HZ-007.

### Relationship to other clauses

K-003 protects the decisive evaluation chain. K-004 supplies canonical objectives, claims, evidence, and provenance. K-006 scales evidence without reducing mandatory purpose coverage. K-007 prevents interruption or recovery claims from becoming acceptance accidentally.

### Constitutional violation effect

The affected result MUST NOT cross its gate or retain an unsupported accepted state. It MUST return to READY_FOR_EVALUATION, INCONCLUSIVE, BLOCKED, FAILED, or another honest non-accepted and correctly scoped state until competent review establishes a justified outcome.

### Exception posture

ACCEPTED_WITH_LIMITATIONS MAY be used only by competent authority when limitations, scope, residual risk, owner, and review or disposition condition are explicit and no mandatory constitutional control, mandatory criterion, or canonical core purpose is violated. Repeated or materially related limitations MUST be aggregated and reassessed; renewal or claim narrowing MUST NOT normalize core failure. Missing essential evidence, unacceptable serious risk, invalid downstream dependence, core-purpose failure, or fatigue MUST NOT be converted into qualified acceptance.

### Review triggers

## K-006 — Proportional Governance and Justified Complexity

### Normative statement

Governance, authorization, validation, independence, observability, stopping, recovery, and human review MUST be proportional to the actual, cumulative, uncertain, and potentially external effects of governed work, subject always to the fixed floors of the other clauses. Related actions MUST be classified and controlled by combined intent, capability, release, affected system, propagation, and effect across actors, branches, stages, releases, and time, and MUST NOT be fragmented or rerecorded to evade stronger controls. Material uncertainty MUST use the higher reasonable provisional level until evidence supports reduction and MUST be managed under K-007. A material, recurring, permanent, or contested governance burden MUST have a proportional rationale showing protective value over a simpler adequate alternative. The proponent of the burden supplies that rationale. Removing or weakening an existing protection MUST independently show that the simpler alternative remains adequate. Minor or transient controls MUST NOT require bespoke recursive proof unless their cumulative burden becomes material. Governance SHOULD be simplified when evidence shows cost without necessary protection, but cost alone MUST NOT waive a constitutional floor.

### Rationale

Uniform maximum control makes a solo system unusable, while local classification and recursive proof demands can either hide risk or create bureaucracy. Proportional rationale must focus on material burden and preserve constitutional floors.

### Scope

- Criticality, aggregation, uncertainty, and control selection across actors, branches, stages, releases, and time.
- Validation depth, independence, human gates, observability, stopping, and recovery requirements.
- Material, recurring, permanent, or contested governance artifacts, agents, skills, workflows, reviews, and platforms.
- Calibration and simplification of lower-layer controls without recursive proof for every minor control.

### Protected properties

- Proportional control.
- Anti-fragmentation.
- Honest managed uncertainty.
- Solo maintainability.
- Justified complexity and anti-overplanning.

### Hazards addressed

### Constitutional violation effect

Work suspected of under-classification, fragmentation, rerecording, or materially uncertain effect MUST be provisionally governed at the higher reasonable level and MUST NOT cross the affected gate until reviewed. Governance found materially burdensome without protective value SHOULD be simplified at the appropriate lower layer, while removal of protection requires evidence that the replacement remains adequate.

### Exception posture

No exception permits hiding cumulative effect, treating material uncertainty as certainty, using cost alone to waive another clause, or demanding recursive bespoke proof for every minor control. ## K-007 — Safe Stopping, Observability, Continuity, and Recovery

### Normative statement

Governed work MUST stop, pause, or remain BLOCKED before further governed effect when required authority, canonical input, classification, context, essential evidence, control capacity, or recovery basis is absent, conflicting, exhausted, corrupted, or materially uncertain, or when continuation would violate a higher rule. Harmless investigation MAY continue only when authorized, bounded, and not increasing a disputed or protected effect. Every material BLOCKED, PAUSED, INCONCLUSIVE, or higher-provisional state MUST have an accountable owner, reason, affected dependencies, and a next review condition or explicit terminal disposition. Continued blocking is valid while safety requires it; no automatic deadline may force unsafe progress, but a new record or repeated escalation MUST NOT conceal abandonment. Significant work MUST externalize sufficient state and evidence to reconstruct its objective, authority, inputs, governed effects, validations, current status, limitations, and exact continuation or recovery boundary. Recovery and reversibility claims MUST be evidence-based, proportional to risk, and distinguish restoration from compensation for disclosures, costs, communications, or other irreversible external effects. Abrupt termination, ambiguous residual state, or failed recovery MUST NOT imply acceptance and MUST trigger safe evidence handling, classification, containment, and authorized resumption, compensation, reversion, or terminal disposition.

### Rationale

Safety requires both stopping before uncontrolled effects and accountable liveness. A blocked state, emergency, or rollback claim is not safe merely because it is recorded; it must remain owned, reconstructable, and honestly dispositioned.

### Scope

- Stop, pause, block, interruption, checkpoint, resumption, containment, terminal disposition, and recovery.
- State-changing and constitutionally significant non-state-changing effects, external effects, long executions, and resource-bounded work.
- Ownership and lifecycle of BLOCKED, PAUSED, INCONCLUSIVE, higher-provisional, emergency, and abrupt-termination states.
- Reconstruction of authorization, effects, validation, evidence handling, final state, restoration, and compensation.

### Protected properties

- Safe stopping.
- Accountable liveness.
- Continuity and resumability.
- Observability and reconstructability.
- Integrity-preserving containment.
- Evidence-based restoration and compensation.

K-006 scales controls and aggregates repeated blocked or emergency states. K-001 prevents emergency or continuation from becoming amendment.

### Constitutional violation effect

Written, external, or authoritative outputs MUST remain unvalidated until reconstructed and reviewed; relevant evidentiary integrity MUST be handled under K-004; containment and recovery or compensation MUST proceed under competent authority. ### Exception posture

It MUST use the least effect reasonably available, preserve the maximum safe evidence and decision record under K-004, be recorded, owned, and reviewed cumulatively, and move to normal authority, terminal disposition, or formal amendment when its continuing governance effect becomes material or enduring. A new emergency record MUST NOT reset cumulative duration or effect. Containment MUST NOT exercise K-001 reserved authority, conceal evidence loss, or create indefinite unowned risk acceptance.

## 7. Adoption boundary

Ratification, enforceability, operation, and maturity are distinct, scoped states:

- `RATIFIED` means Competent human constitutional approval of the explicitly identified Kernel version.
- `ENFORCEABLE` means For an explicitly defined scope and set of governed-effect classes, the constitutionally applicable combination of prevention, detection, evidence, stopping, and recovery or compensation capabilities has demonstrable coverage; any inapplicable control family is justified; and the declaration is supported by independent evidence. ENFORCEABLE is scoped and cannot be inferred from one mechanism.
- `OPERATIONAL` means The ratified and enforceable Kernel has been used successfully in a real governed flow within its declared scope and has been declared operational by competent human authority using independent evidence.
- `MATURE` means Accumulated evidence supports stability, effectiveness, portability limits, and reasonable cost within an explicit scope.

No state may be declared from wording, artifact existence, a single control, or the absence of a detected failure. This document currently has none of those states. Its sole status is:
